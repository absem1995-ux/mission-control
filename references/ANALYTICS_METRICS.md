# Analytics Metrics & Interpretation Guide

## Core Metrics (What We Track)

### Universal Metrics (All Platforms)

| Metric | What It Means | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------------|---|
| **Views** | How many people saw the post | Raw reach (vanity metric) |
| **Engagement Rate** | (Likes + Comments + Shares) / Views | Quality of content (not just reach) |
| **Completion Rate** | % of viewers who watched til end | Hook strength + content quality |
| **Save Rate** | % of viewers who saved/bookmarked | Content perceived as valuable |
| **Share Rate** | % of viewers who shared | Network effect potential |
| **CTR (Click-Through Rate)** | % who clicked link/button | Conversion potential |
| **Comments** | Total comments on post | Audience interest level |

---

### Platform-Specific Metrics

**TikTok:**
- Watch Time (total minutes watched)

**Reddit:**
- Upvotes (equivalent to likes)
- Comments (engagement indicator)
- Award count
- Share count
- Traffic to external links

**Facebook:**
- Reach (unique people)
- Engagement (reactions, comments, shares)
- Video views
- Click-through rate

## Interpreting Performance

### Green Flags (Perform Well)

**High Views + High Engagement Rate:**
- Indicates good hook + quality content
- Algorithm will push more
- Hook is working; double down

**High Completion Rate (70%+):**
- Content keeps people watching til end
- Hook is strong
- Pacing is good

**High Save Rate (3%+):**
- People find content valuable
- More important than likes (indicates utility)
- Good for long-term visibility

**High Share Rate (2%+):**
- Network effect; people tell friends
- Algorithm boost potential
- Viral trajectory indicator

---

### Red Flags (Perform Poorly)

**Low Engagement Rate (<2%):**
- Hook isn't resonating
- Content quality issue
- Try different hook next time

**Low Completion Rate (<50%):**
- Hook fails to hold attention
- Pacing too slow or too fast
- First 3 seconds failing

**High Views + Low Saves:**
- People interested but don't see value
- Content entertaining but not useful
- Adjust positioning

**High Comments + Low Follows:**
- People engaging but not convinced to follow
- CTA weak
- Content not relevant to audience goals

## Hook Performance Analysis

### Tracking Hooks

Each hook gets tested. We track:
1. **Initial performance** (first 3 posts with this hook)
2. **Trend** (improving or declining?)
3. **Category winner** (which narratives/tutorials/showcases work best?)
4. **Platform winner** (which hook works best on TikTok vs LinkedIn?)

### Decision Rules

**Double Down (proven winners):**
- 3+ posts with >80% average engagement
- Completion rate >75%
- Positive trend over time

**Keep Testing:**
- 1-2 posts
- Mixed results (some good, some bad)
- Needs more data

**Stop Using (dropped):**
- <50% engagement rate
- Declining trend (each post worse)
- Low completion rate

### What You'll See Each Morning

**Example Daily Report:**
```

Yesterday: 156,420 views | 8.2% engagement | 3 posts published

1. narrative_001 (TikTok) — 48K views, 12% engagement

📉 Underperformers:
- tutorial_002 (LinkedIn) — 850 views, 1.2% engagement
  → Recommendation: Skip this hook, try different angle

💡 Insights:
- Narratives outperforming tutorials 3.2x
- TikTok 7:30am slot strong; 9pm weaker
- Saves increased 15% (people finding content valuable)
- Comment sentiment positive (check for common questions)

🎯 Today's Actions:
1. Generate 3 new posts using narrative patterns
2. Skip tutorial hook, try showcase instead
3. Post earlier on TikTok (shift 9pm to 2pm)
```

### What to Do With This Info

**If narrative hooks dominate:** Keep using them, reduce tutorials

**If tutorial hooks get high saves:** People want educational content; increase mix

**If engagement declining overall:** Hook fatigue; rotate in new hooks or refresh positioning

**If one platform lagging:** Check posting time, content type, CTA; consider reducing frequency or stopping

## Common Mistakes in Analytics Interpretation

### ❌ Mistake 1: Chasing Vanity Metrics
- **Wrong:** "Post got 50K views, so it's great"
- **Right:** "Post got 50K views but only 2% engagement; hook works, content doesn't"

### ❌ Mistake 2: Judging Too Early
- **Wrong:** "1 post with low views = hook is bad"
- **Right:** "Need 3+ posts with a hook before deciding"

### ❌ Mistake 3: Ignoring Platform Differences
- **Wrong:** "LinkedIn post got 800 views, that's low"
- **Right:** "LinkedIn post got 800 views with 5% engagement = excellent for this platform"

### ❌ Mistake 4: Not Tracking Trends
- **Wrong:** "Post A got 5K views, Post B got 4K views; both equal"
- **Right:** "Post A stable 5K; Post B declining 6K→5K→4K; A is healthier"

### ❌ Mistake 5: Ignoring Qualitative Feedback
- **Wrong:** Only looking at numbers
- **Right:** Reading comments, noting questions, identifying content gaps

## A/B Testing Setup (Phase 2)

Once you have baseline data, test variations:

### Hook A/B Test
- Same image, different hook text
- Track which hook performs better
- Keep winner, drop loser

### Platform A/B Test
- Same content on different platforms
- Track performance per platform
- Allocate effort accordingly

### Posting Time A/B Test
- Same hook at different times
- Track optimal posting window
- Adjust schedule

### CTA A/B Test
- Same content, different CTAs
- Track which drives most action
- Use winner going forward
