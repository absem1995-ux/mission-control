# Platform Guidelines for Atlas

This document provides platform-specific best practices for content creation and posting.

## TikTok

**Account Warmup:**
- Required for new accounts: 7-14 days of natural usage (30-60 min/day)
- Activities: Scroll FYP, like videos, follow creators, watch full videos, leave comments
- Signal ready: FYP dominated by your niche content

**Content Specs:**
- Format: 9:16 (vertical)
- Duration: 15-60 seconds optimal (can go up to 10 minutes)
- Best practice: Under 30 seconds for maximum completion

**Posting Strategy:**
- Frequency: 3-5x daily optimal
- Best times: 6-10am, 4-6pm, 10-12pm local time
- Audio critical: 40% of algorithm weight on sound selection

**What Works:**
- Trending sounds (MUST add manually before publishing)
- Hook-heavy first 3 seconds
- Stories/narratives (before/after patterns)
- Quick cuts + visual motion
- Text overlays for retention

**What Doesn't:**
- Low-quality audio
- Watermarks from other platforms
- No call-to-action
- Boring first 3 seconds

**Publishing Note:**
- Posts go to SELF_ONLY drafts via API
- You must manually select audio from TikTok's library
- Then publish from your inbox
- This is a TikTok API limitation (not our choice)

## Instagram Reels

**Content Specs:**
- Format: 9:16 (vertical), but 1:1 and 4:5 supported
- Duration: 15-90 seconds
- Best length: 21-34 seconds (optimal engagement)

**Posting Strategy:**
- Frequency: 1-2 Reels/day + 1-2 Stories
- Best times: 11am-1pm, 5-7pm (timezone aware)
- Hashtags: 3-5 niche + 5-10 broad

**What Works:**
- Reels get 40-67% more engagement than feed posts
- Trending sounds (Instagram Audio Library)
- Text overlays + captions
- Multi-scene editing
- Clear CTA

**What Doesn't:**
- Feed posts without video
- Untrended audio
- Inconsistent posting
- Invisible CTAs

---

## YouTube Shorts

**Content Specs:**
- Format: 9:16 (vertical)
- Duration: 15-60 seconds
- File size: Up to 256GB (for longer videos)

**Posting Strategy:**
- Frequency: 1x daily to 3x/week
- Best times: Friday 6pm-9pm, Saturday/Sunday 10am-3pm
- Hashtags: 2-3 relevant

**What Works:**
- Shorts algorithm separate from regular video algorithm
- Trending sounds
- Quick cuts, motion
- Educational + entertaining mix
- Clear hook (first 3 seconds)

**What Doesn't:**
- Watermarks
- Poor audio
- Long intros

**Note:** YouTube Shorts have MASSIVE potential in 2026 (less saturated than TikTok).

## LinkedIn

**Content Specs:**
- Format: 1:1 (square) or 4:5 (portrait)
- Duration for videos: Any (no strict limit)
- Text: No character limit, but 200 chars for preview

**Posting Strategy:**
- Frequency: 1-3x per week (less is more)
- Best days: Tuesday-Thursday
- Best times: 8-10am, 12pm, 5-6pm
- Engagement window: First 1 hour critical

**What Works:**
- Thought leadership content
- Educational posts
- Industry insights
- Document posts (PDFs, case studies)
- Comments/shares drive algorithm (unlike TikTok)
- Professional tone with personality

**What Doesn't:**
- Overt sales pitches
- Unrelated memes
- Hashtag stuffing
- Poor video quality

**LinkedIn-Specific Notes:**
- Document posts perform 3-5x better than text
- Comments = weighted higher than likes
- Shares indicate algorithm boost
- Takes 24-48 hours for full analytics

## Reddit

**Account Requirements:**
- New accounts: 1-week old minimum
- Karma: 50+ comment karma (for posting in most communities)
- Account reputation: Reddit watches for spam/bot behavior

**Community Rules:**
- Every subreddit has different rules
- Self-promotion heavily restricted
- Read "About" section before posting
- Violating community rules = instant removal + potential ban

**Content Specs:**
- Title: 300 characters max
- Text: Any length
- Images: Community-dependent (usually 5-20MB max)
- Video: Community-dependent

**Posting Strategy:**
- Frequency: 1-2x per week (less is better)
- Best times: 10am-2pm UTC (US business hours)
- Timing: Monday/Tuesday for max visibility

**What Works:**
- Genuine, helpful advice
- Niche expertise
- Funny/entertaining content (depends on subreddit)
- Authentic voice (no sales language)
- Engagement in comments

**What Doesn't:**
- Spam/bot posts (instant removal)
- Links without context
- Overt self-promotion
- Cross-posting (perceived as spam)
- Posting same content to multiple subreddits

**Warning:** Reddit is **anti-automation**. Accounts that smell automated get permanently banned. Use cautiously.

## Facebook

**Content Specs:**
- Feed posts: 1200 x 628px (16:9)
- Stories: 1080 x 1920px (9:16)
- Reels: 1080 x 1920px (9:16)
- Video: Up to 4 hours, 4GB max

**Posting Strategy:**
- Frequency: 1-2x daily (pages, unlike personal accounts)
- Best times: Wednesday-Thursday, 1-3pm
- Engagement window: First 1 hour

**What Works:**
- Reels perform 3-5x better than feed posts
- Video > image > text
- Stories for casual engagement
- Community interaction (comments, shares)
- Clear CTA

**What Doesn't:**
- Text-only posts (low reach)
- Misleading links
- Clickbait headlines
- Poor video quality

## Cross-Platform Content Adaptation

### TikTok → Instagram
- Remove TikTok watermarks
- Add Instagram-style text overlays
- Same video works (9:16 format matches)

### TikTok → YouTube Shorts
- Can use same video
- Add YouTube-specific CTAs (Subscribe, watch full video)
- Add YouTube end screens

### TikTok → LinkedIn
- Crop to 1:1 or 4:5
- Change captions to professional tone
- Emphasize business value, not entertainment
- Add document context if applicable

### TikTok → Reddit
- Convert to MP4 (no watermarks)
- Add context/explanation in title + first comment
- Be honest it's cross-posted (Reddit values authenticity)

### TikTok → Facebook
- Same video works
- Adjust text for older demographic
- Longer copy (Facebook users read more)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Using trending sounds that are copyrighted** (YouTube Copyright Strike)
2. **Not testing content before full rollout** (data drives decisions)
3. **Ignoring platform-specific algorithms** (each platform is different)
4. **No clear CTA** (people don't know what to do)
5. **Inconsistent posting** (algorithm prefers consistency)
6. **Copying other creators verbatim** (shows zero originality)
7. **Forgetting account warmup** (new accounts get throttled)
8. **Watermarks from other platforms** (indicates lack of ownership)
9. **Poor audio quality** (kills retention)
10. **Posting at wrong times** (algorithm ignores posts outside peak hours)
